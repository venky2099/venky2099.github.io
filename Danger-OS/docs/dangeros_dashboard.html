§1 THE STORY
"Eleven consecutive confirmations of the Bhaya Quiescence Law — then the defence question the series had been building toward. What if fear, wisdom, trust, and aliveness could watch a machine the way they watch a mind? Danger-OS is the answer."
Danger-OS · Maya-Defence Series Paper 1 · Nexus Learning Labs, Bengaluru · April 2026

The Maya series built a complete Advaita Vedanta Antahkarana across twelve papers — nine spiking neural network papers and three post-series extensions ending in Maya-LLM. Every one confirmed a single structural law: Bhaya, the fear primitive, does not drift toward chronic escalation. It stays quiescent until something genuinely dangerous happens, then responds, then returns to rest.

Danger-OS asks the defence question this law makes possible: what happens when the same four-neuron architecture watches an operating system instead of a training run? Across 5,710 ticks of continuous operation spanning 8 scenarios — including a 45-minute pure-idle test and adversarial voltage-injection stress — the affective engine produced 18 terminal action decisions and zero processes were actually terminated. The consent barrier held. The Bhaya Quiescence Law held. The architecture is restrained not by rules, but by the shape of its own mind.

§2 WHAT IS DANGER-OS?
Four Neurons Watching an Operating System
Plain language · no ML background needed

Imagine a security guard who is architecturally incapable of shooting. They can see threats. They can raise alarms. They can choose to restrain someone — but the act of actually doing harm requires a physical key that only someone else holds. That separation is not a rule they follow. It is the shape of the role itself.

Danger-OS equips a Windows process monitor with four Leaky Integrate-and-Fire neurons — Bhaya (fear, τ=3), Vairagya (wisdom, τ=20), Shraddha (trust, τ=10), and Spanda (aliveness, τ=5) — that continuously read CPU, RAM, and process enumeration signals. Every 500ms the four voltages update, propagate through a shared 4×4 weight matrix, and produce one of five action classes: IDLE, PROTECT, ALERT, SUSPEND, or KILL. No hardcoded rules. The behaviour emerges from voltage ratios.

# Architecture: Four LIF neurons · 500ms tick · psutil backend
dV/dt = -V/τ + I(t) // leaky integrate-and-fire
V ≥ threshold → fire, reset to 0
action = arbiter(V_bhaya, V_vairagya, V_shraddha, V_spanda)

# Safety contract (non-negotiable)
execute(action) = arbiter_decision AND consent_barrier_cleared()
consent_barrier_cleared() = False // dry-run default across all 8 runs

The arbiter and the executor are architecturally decoupled. The arbiter can choose KILL. The executor refuses to perform it until a consent barrier has been explicitly cleared by the operator — and in every one of the 5,710 ticks we report here, that barrier remained engaged. The arbiter produced 18 terminal decisions. Zero processes were actually terminated.

The distinction between SUSPEND and KILL is where the Vairagya (wisdom) primitive does architectural work. At tick 1916 of Run F, Bhaya hit 0.638 with Vairagya at 0.340 — the arbiter produced SUSPEND. One tick later, Vairagya reset to 0 after firing. Bhaya read 0.613 — the arbiter produced KILL. Same Bhaya ceiling. Different action. The difference was one neuron's availability.

§6 SERIES CONSTANTS · NOW IN DEFENCE
What the Substrate Cannot Change

Two empirical constants confirmed across the Maya research series now appear in a defence operating system. These are not coincidences and they are not hyperparameter choices. They are structural properties of the Antahkarana architecture — visible wherever the four-neuron design is instantiated, regardless of what it is watching.

BHAYA QUIESCENCE LAW · 11TH CONSECUTIVE CONFIRMATION
CONFIRMED
Aggregate terminal action rate 0.315% across 5,710 ticks (18 terminal actions / 5,710 ticks). The rate stayed bounded across scenario durations of 3 to 45 minutes, across pure idle to adversarial stress, and across scripted threats to real user workflow. Confirmed in P1–P9 under SNN replay, in Maya-mPCI under continuous observation, in Maya-LLM under transformer fine-tuning. Now confirmed in a defence OS under live process enumeration. The architecture produces restraint as a structural property, not a tuning parameter.
WISDOM-MEDIATED ARBITRATION · NEW DEFENCE-CONTEXT FINDING
FIRST SHOWN
The mechanistic distinction between SUSPEND and KILL emerges from Vairagya's instantaneous availability at the decision tick, not from any threshold on Bhaya. This is first shown in defence context in Run F (tick 1916→1917, Bhaya 0.638→0.613, Vairagya 0.340→0.000, SUSPEND→KILL). It predicts that a damaged wisdom primitive (injury, exhaustion, adversarial suppression of Vairagya) would produce a Vairagya-null pathology — a defence system that can only KILL, never SUSPEND. This is a concrete failure mode to design against in future iterations.
